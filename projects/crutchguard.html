CrutchGuard – Integrated Crutch Attachment

  • Relevant Skills: SolidWorks, GD&T, Product Development, Iterative Design, Rapid Prototyping/3D Printing, Mechanical Assembly & Testing, DFM, Technical Documentation

Background

Annually, over 575,000 Americans are prescribed crutches, yet more than 70,000 crutch-related injuries occur, often from slips on wet or uneven surfaces. These secondary accidents can cause reinjury or new harm. For our Engineering 29 final project, my team designed CrutchGuard, an integrated crutch-foot attachment that increases contact area, improves traction, and allows controlled rotation to better approximate natural foot motion.

Goals

The project required us to:
  • Develop a functional mechanical prototype that could attach to a standard crutch shaft
  • Apply GD&T and fit selection to critical rotating and clamping interfaces
  • Prototype using 3D-printed components, threaded inserts, fasteners, and off-the-shelf grip material
  • Evaluate assembly behavior, stability improvements, and design refinements through physical testing

Design and Execution

  • Design and Components:
    • Component A (Base): Wide platform with anti-slip rubber to increase ground contact area and reduce local pressure on uneven terrain
    • Component B (Support): Rounded support geometry that permits controlled rotation for smoother gait mechanics
    • Component C (Clamp): Adjustable clamping feature that transfers load from the crutch shaft into the attachment
    • Component D (Lock Mechanism): Mechanical interface between clamp and leg that constrains separation while preserving rotational freedom
    • Component E (Leg Connector): Extendable connector concept sized for future height adjustment across different users
  • Tolerances & Fits:
    • Specified interference (LN1) and sliding (RC5) fits across critical interfaces (e.g., Base–Support, Support–Leg) to ensure both secure locking and necessary motion
  • Prototyping:
    • Fabricated parts with PLA using a Bambu P1S 3D printer
    • Integrated off-the-shelf bolts, locknuts, heat inserts, and threaded nuts
  • Reflection and Iteration:
    • Grip layer added to base significantly improved traction
    • Locking mechanism redesigned in concept phase for quick detachment without tools
    • Proposed curved base shape for smoother walking motion, better simulating foot roll

Outcome

  • Produced a working prototype that increased crutch-foot stability and traction
  • Validated selected fits through assembly, rotation, and component interaction
  • Identified next design improvements: tool-less detachment, adjustable height locking, and an ergonomic curved base for smoother rollover
Despite initial challenges in fabrication and tolerance control, the CrutchGuard met its primary goal: reducing reinjury risk for crutch users.
