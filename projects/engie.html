ENGIE Sustainability Project – Livermore High

  • Relevant Skills: Energy Systems Analysis, Cost-Benefit and Life-Cycle Analysis (LEED BD+C), Sustainability Planning, Technical Communication

Background

In partnership with ENGIE, Livermore High School executed a multi-system sustainability project to reduce energy costs, modernize campus infrastructure, and expose students to applied renewable-energy systems. The project combined lighting retrofits, solar array installation, and HVAC upgrades, creating a useful case study in building energy performance, life-cycle cost savings, and facility-level decarbonization.

Goals

Project goals included:
  • Reduce annual campus energy consumption through lighting, HVAC, and distributed-generation upgrades
  • Lower operational costs and reinvest savings into education
  • Improve indoor comfort, safety, and learning conditions through LEED BD+C-aligned analysis
  • Evaluate a scalable retrofit model for district-wide adoption
  • Translate technical system upgrades into clear sustainability education for students and the broader community

Design and Execution

  • Lighting Retrofit
    • Replaced 281 outdated bulbs and installed 220 motion-controlled fixtures
    • Improved illumination quality while reducing glare and unnecessary runtime
    • Integrated occupancy sensing to reduce wasted energy in low-use areas
  • Solar Installation
    • Installed three solar arrays, including a 178.2 kW system in the stadium parking lot
    • Projected annual production of ~295,465 kWh, reducing grid dependence
    • Supported long-term savings analysis and potential value from excess energy resale
  • HVAC Upgrades
    • Replaced aging electrical and HVAC infrastructure to improve system efficiency
    • Improved indoor air quality, humidity control, and thermal comfort
    • Reduced annual HVAC energy use by an estimated 78,221 kWh

Outcome

  • Lighting: ~345,000 kWh/year reduction and $89,380 annual savings
  • Solar: ~$70,403 annual savings
  • HVAC: ~$18,364 annual savings
  • Total Annual Savings: $178,148 (~$1.78M over 10 years)
  • Documented significant carbon-footprint and greenhouse-gas reductions from combined efficiency and solar upgrades
