eVTOL: Tiltrotor & Landing Gear

Summary: Designed and fabricated the tiltrotor mechanism and landing gear for a ¼-scale eVTOL prototype. Leveraged SolidWorks and Fusion360 for CAD and analysis, integrating structural and aerodynamic constraints into a manufacturable design. System incorporated FAA-compliant load case validation and weight-optimized carbon-fiber structures for improved efficiency.

Requirements:

  • Support FAA-compliant load cases for tiltrotor and landing gear systems
  • Validate structural integrity under worst-case loading scenarios
  • Maintain low weight while ensuring safety margins
  • Enhance overall flight efficiency through optimized design

Main Components:

  • Tiltrotor Mechanism: Modeled and fabricated mechanism enabling variable rotor positioning for vertical and horizontal flight modes. Verified strength and deflection performance through FEA simulations.
  • Landing Gear: Developed robust carbon-fiber landing gear capable of absorbing impact loads during touchdown. Designed geometry to minimize weight while achieving ±15% mass reduction compared to baseline aluminum designs.

Key Outcomes:

  • Achieved FAA-compliant validation for structural performance
  • Reduced mass of carbon-fiber components by 15%, improving endurance and efficiency
  • Ensured safe, reliable operation for ¼-scale prototype under test conditions

Skills: CAD & FEA (SolidWorks, Fusion360), Carbon-Fiber Design, FAA Load Case Analysis, Materials Optimization, Prototyping & Manufacturing
